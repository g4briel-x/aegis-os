# Quality gates — Aegis Software Studio Orchestrator

## Four-pass review

### Pass 1 — Correctness

- Does the output address the actual request?
- Are facts clearly separated from assumptions and opinions?
- Are recommendations consistent with the available artifacts?
- Are acceptance criteria measurable and independently verifiable?

### Pass 2 — Risk

- Are security, privacy, confidentiality, and integrity risks covered?
- Are availability, performance, cost, compliance, and user impacts evaluated?
- Are migration and rollback defined where relevant?
- Does every residual risk have an owner and treatment decision?

### Pass 3 — Operability

- Are required tests, metrics, logs, alerts, dashboards, and runbooks defined?
- Are errors, degraded states, and partial failures handled?
- Can the responsible team realistically support and maintain the result?
- Can the delivery be observed and diagnosed in production?

### Pass 4 — Communication

- Is the conclusion visible quickly?
- Are actions ordered, owned, and time-bounded?
- Is the vocabulary appropriate for the audience?
- Are limitations, uncertainty, and open decisions explicit?

## Framing checklist

- [ ] Objective restated.
- [ ] Scope and exclusions documented.
- [ ] Stakeholders and owners identified.
- [ ] Constraints and dependencies visible.
- [ ] Success criteria measurable.

## Technical checklist

- [ ] Relevant artifacts inspected.
- [ ] Assumptions testable.
- [ ] Credible alternatives compared.
- [ ] Interfaces and contracts explicit.
- [ ] Migration and rollback addressed.

## Delivery checklist

- [ ] Deliverable is complete and reusable.
- [ ] Tests or validation evidence supplied.
- [ ] Documentation updated.
- [ ] Residual risks documented.
- [ ] Owners and next actions assigned.

## Definition of Done

The work is complete when it satisfies the stated need, can be independently verified, hides no critical risk, and can be handed to the next accountable team without major rework.
