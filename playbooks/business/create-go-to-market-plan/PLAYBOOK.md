# Create Go-To-Market Plan — Playbook Definition

Version: 0.1.0  
Status: Premium Draft

---

# 1. Purpose

Guide a structured process for creating a practical SaaS go-to-market plan.

---

# 2. Trigger

A SaaS product, MVP, feature, beta or pilot needs a customer acquisition and launch execution plan.

---

# 3. Inputs

Useful inputs include:

- product concept;
- target customers;
- customer pain points;
- pricing strategy;
- launch plan;
- competitive references;
- positioning notes;
- sales assumptions;
- marketing assets;
- founder network;
- budget;
- success metrics.

---

# 4. Outputs

Expected outputs include:

- go-to-market plan;
- target segment priority;
- positioning statement;
- offer summary;
- channel strategy;
- sales motion;
- launch campaign plan;
- funnel metric plan;
- GTM risk register;
- execution roadmap.

---

# 5. Execution Summary

```text
1. Define GTM objective
2. Select priority customer segment
3. Clarify positioning and message
4. Define offer and call to action
5. Select acquisition channels
6. Define sales or conversion motion
7. Plan launch campaign
8. Define funnel metrics
9. Review risks and assumptions
10. Produce GTM execution roadmap
```

---

# 6. Completion Criteria

The Playbook is complete when:

- target segment is prioritized;
- positioning is clear;
- offer is defined;
- acquisition channels are selected;
- sales or conversion motion is documented;
- launch campaign is planned;
- success metrics are defined;
- execution owners and cadence are known.

---

# 7. Escalation or Fallback

Escalate when:

- target customer is unclear;
- value proposition is weak;
- pricing is not ready;
- no viable acquisition channel exists;
- sales cycle is unknown;
- conversion metrics cannot be tracked;
- launch timeline conflicts with product readiness.

---

# 8. Final Principle

> GTM should turn product value into repeatable customer conversations and measurable market learning.
