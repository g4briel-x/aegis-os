Version: 0.1.0  
Status: Premium Draft

# 1. Example — Audiovisual Financing SaaS GTM

## Trigger

A SaaS platform helps creators prepare film, series and documentary projects for financing.

## Expected Execution

The Playbook should guide the team to:

- target independent producers and creators first;
- position the product around financing readiness;
- offer private beta or paid pilot;
- use founder-led outreach and industry communities;
- track demos, pilots and project submissions.

## Expected Output

```text
GTM plan
Target segment priority
Positioning statement
Channel strategy
Launch campaign plan
Funnel metric plan
Execution roadmap
```

# 2. Example — B2B SaaS Private Beta

## Trigger

A SaaS MVP needs 20 qualified beta users.

## Expected Execution

The Playbook should guide the team to:

- define ideal beta customer;
- write outreach message;
- select LinkedIn and direct email;
- define beta acceptance criteria;
- track activation and feedback.

---

# 3. Example — Enterprise Pilot GTM

## Trigger

A product needs its first enterprise pilot.

## Expected Execution

The Playbook should guide the team to:

- define target account list;
- create pilot offer;
- prepare demo script;
- define pilot success metrics;
- track sales objections.

---

# 4. Example — Self-Serve SaaS Launch

## Trigger

A product launches with self-serve signup.

## Expected Execution

The Playbook should guide the team to:

- define landing page message;
- choose acquisition channels;
- track signup and activation;
- review conversion after launch sprint.

# 5. Final Principle

> Examples show that GTM planning connects the product promise to a repeatable path toward customers.
