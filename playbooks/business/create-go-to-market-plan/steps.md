# Create Go-To-Market Plan — Steps

Version: 0.1.0  
Status: Premium Draft

---

# 1. Step 1 — Define GTM Objective

Clarify the business goal of the GTM plan.

Examples:

```text
validate demand
acquire first 10 customers
fill private beta
launch paid pilot
generate qualified demos
convert free users to paid
```

Capture:

- objective;
- timeline;
- target number;
- owner;
- constraints.

Output:

```text
GTM objective statement
```

---

# 2. Step 2 — Select Priority Customer Segment

Choose the first segment to target.

Review:

- pain urgency;
- budget availability;
- access to buyers;
- segment size;
- sales complexity;
- product fit;
- willingness to pay.

Output:

```text
Target segment priority
```

---

# 3. Step 3 — Clarify Positioning and Message

Define why the product matters.

Positioning should explain:

- target customer;
- painful problem;
- product category;
- unique value;
- proof or credibility;
- outcome.

Output:

```text
Positioning statement
```

---

# 4. Step 4 — Define Offer and Call to Action

Create a concrete offer for the customer.

Offer types:

- free beta access;
- paid pilot;
- demo request;
- early access waitlist;
- limited launch discount;
- consultation call;
- enterprise pilot.

Output:

```text
Offer and CTA summary
```

---

# 5. Step 5 — Select Acquisition Channels

Choose channels based on customer behavior and available resources.

Possible channels:

- founder-led outreach;
- LinkedIn content;
- email outreach;
- communities;
- partnerships;
- webinars;
- SEO;
- paid ads;
- events;
- referrals.

Output:

```text
Channel strategy
```

---

# 6. Step 6 — Define Sales or Conversion Motion

Define how prospects become customers.

Sales motions:

- self-serve signup;
- demo-led sales;
- founder-led sales;
- product-led growth;
- paid pilot;
- enterprise sales.

Capture:

- funnel steps;
- qualification criteria;
- demo flow;
- objection handling;
- follow-up cadence.

Output:

```text
Sales and conversion motion
```

---

# 7. Step 7 — Plan Launch Campaign

Define launch execution.

Capture:

- launch audience;
- announcement channels;
- content assets;
- outreach list;
- launch timeline;
- responsibilities;
- follow-up plan.

Output:

```text
Launch campaign plan
```

---

# 8. Step 8 — Define Funnel Metrics

Define what success looks like.

Track:

- reach;
- visits;
- signups;
- demos booked;
- qualified leads;
- pilots started;
- conversions;
- revenue;
- activation;
- retention.

Output:

```text
GTM funnel metric plan
```

---

# 9. Step 9 — Review Risks and Assumptions

Identify weak assumptions before execution.

Common risks:

- wrong segment;
- weak message;
- low willingness to pay;
- poor channel fit;
- long sales cycle;
- low activation;
- product not ready;
- support overload.

Output:

```text
GTM risk register
```

---

# 10. Step 10 — Produce Execution Roadmap

Turn the plan into execution.

Include:

- weekly actions;
- owners;
- assets to create;
- outreach targets;
- metric review cadence;
- decision checkpoints.

Output:

```text
GTM execution roadmap
```

---

# 11. Final Principle

> GTM steps should move the team from market theory to measurable customer acquisition activity.
