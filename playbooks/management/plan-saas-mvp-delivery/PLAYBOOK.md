# Plan SaaS MVP Delivery — Playbook Definition

Version: 0.1.0  
Status: Premium Draft

---

# 1. Purpose

Guide a structured process for turning SaaS MVP definition into an executable delivery plan.

---

# 2. Trigger

A SaaS MVP has enough product and technical definition to begin delivery planning.

---

# 3. Inputs

Useful inputs include:

- MVP definition;
- PRD;
- UX flow;
- architecture plan;
- feature list;
- technical constraints;
- team capacity;
- target launch date;
- risk register;
- stakeholder priorities;
- budget constraints.

---

# 4. Outputs

Expected outputs include:

- MVP delivery plan;
- workstream map;
- milestone roadmap;
- prioritized backlog;
- dependency map;
- ownership matrix;
- risk register;
- delivery cadence;
- release readiness plan;
- launch checklist.

---

# 5. Execution Summary

```text
1. Confirm MVP delivery objective
2. Review available product and architecture inputs
3. Split work into delivery workstreams
4. Define milestones and outcomes
5. Convert scope into backlog groups
6. Map dependencies and blockers
7. Assign owners and responsibilities
8. Identify delivery risks
9. Define cadence and reporting model
10. Produce launch-ready delivery plan
```

---

# 6. Completion Criteria

The Playbook is complete when:

- delivery objective is clear;
- workstreams are defined;
- milestones have outcomes;
- dependencies are visible;
- owners are assigned;
- risks are documented;
- delivery cadence is defined;
- release path is clear;
- next execution step is known.

---

# 7. Escalation or Fallback

Escalate when:

- MVP scope is unstable;
- architecture is not ready;
- UX flow is incomplete;
- security requirements are unresolved;
- delivery timeline is unrealistic;
- ownership is unclear;
- key dependencies have no owner.

---

# 8. Final Principle

> Delivery planning turns product ambition into coordinated execution.
