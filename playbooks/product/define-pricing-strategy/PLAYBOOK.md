# Define Pricing Strategy — Playbook Definition

Version: 0.1.0  
Status: Premium Draft

---

# 1. Purpose

Guide a structured process for defining SaaS pricing, packaging and monetization assumptions.

---

# 2. Trigger

A SaaS product, MVP or feature set needs pricing before launch, sales, fundraising or monetization validation.

---

# 3. Inputs

Useful inputs include:

- product concept;
- target customers;
- use cases;
- feature list;
- customer pain points;
- value proposition;
- competitor pricing;
- cost structure;
- market positioning;
- sales motion;
- growth goals;
- customer interviews.

---

# 4. Outputs

Expected outputs include:

- pricing strategy;
- customer segment map;
- value driver map;
- pricing metric recommendation;
- tier structure;
- packaging model;
- free trial or freemium decision;
- willingness-to-pay assumptions;
- pricing experiment plan;
- launch pricing recommendation.

---

# 5. Execution Summary

```text
1. Define customer segments
2. Identify value drivers
3. Select pricing metric
4. Define packaging logic
5. Design pricing tiers
6. Review free trial or freemium option
7. Benchmark competitor references
8. Check cost and margin assumptions
9. Define validation experiments
10. Produce pricing recommendation
```

---

# 6. Completion Criteria

The Playbook is complete when:

- customer segments are defined;
- pricing metric is selected;
- value drivers are mapped;
- tiers are structured;
- packaging is clear;
- pricing risks are documented;
- validation plan is defined;
- launch recommendation is ready.

---

# 7. Escalation or Fallback

Escalate when:

- customer segments are unclear;
- willingness to pay is unknown;
- cost to serve may exceed price;
- pricing could block adoption;
- enterprise sales motion requires negotiation;
- competitor pricing is highly variable;
- legal, tax or payment constraints apply.

---

# 8. Final Principle

> Pricing is a strategy, not just a number. It defines who the product serves and how value becomes revenue.
