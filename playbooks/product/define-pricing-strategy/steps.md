# Define Pricing Strategy — Steps

Version: 0.1.0  
Status: Premium Draft

---

# 1. Step 1 — Define Customer Segments

Identify who will pay and why.

Segment by:

- company size;
- user type;
- budget owner;
- urgency;
- use case;
- value received;
- willingness to pay;
- sales motion.

Output:

```text
Customer segment map
```

---

# 2. Step 2 — Identify Value Drivers

Define what creates measurable value for each segment.

Examples:

- time saved;
- revenue gained;
- risk reduced;
- workflow automated;
- compliance improved;
- collaboration improved;
- funding accelerated;
- cost avoided.

Output:

```text
Value driver map
```

---

# 3. Step 3 — Select Pricing Metric

Choose what pricing scales with.

Possible metrics:

- per user;
- per workspace;
- per project;
- per usage volume;
- per seat plus usage;
- per transaction;
- flat subscription;
- enterprise contract.

Evaluate:

- customer understanding;
- value alignment;
- predictability;
- cost alignment;
- implementation complexity.

Output:

```text
Pricing metric recommendation
```

---

# 4. Step 4 — Define Packaging Logic

Decide how capabilities are grouped.

Packaging options:

- feature-based tiers;
- usage-based limits;
- role-based access;
- support-level packaging;
- workflow-based packaging;
- platform plus add-ons.

Output:

```text
Packaging model
```

---

# 5. Step 5 — Design Pricing Tiers

Create clear tiers.

Common structure:

```text
Starter
Professional
Business
Enterprise
```

For each tier, define:

- target customer;
- included features;
- limits;
- support level;
- price hypothesis;
- upgrade trigger.

Output:

```text
Tier structure
```

---

# 6. Step 6 — Review Free Trial, Freemium or Demo-Led Model

Choose acquisition and conversion path.

Options:

- free trial;
- freemium;
- paid pilot;
- demo-led sales;
- usage-based trial;
- invite-only beta.

Output:

```text
Trial and access model decision
```

---

# 7. Step 7 — Benchmark Competitor References

Use competitors as reference, not as the only source of truth.

Review:

- competitor tiers;
- pricing metrics;
- free trial models;
- enterprise packaging;
- value claims;
- market positioning.

Output:

```text
Competitive pricing reference
```

---

# 8. Step 8 — Check Cost and Margin Assumptions

Verify that pricing can support the business.

Review:

- infrastructure cost;
- support cost;
- onboarding cost;
- payment fees;
- AI or API cost;
- gross margin target;
- sales cost.

Output:

```text
Unit economics check
```

---

# 9. Step 9 — Define Validation Experiments

Plan how pricing assumptions will be tested.

Examples:

- landing page pricing test;
- discovery interview pricing questions;
- paid pilot offer;
- sales call objection tracking;
- willingness-to-pay survey;
- A/B packaging test.

Output:

```text
Pricing validation experiment plan
```

---

# 10. Step 10 — Produce Pricing Recommendation

Create a clear pricing decision for launch.

Include:

- target segments;
- pricing metric;
- tiers;
- trial model;
- assumptions;
- risks;
- validation plan;
- next review date.

Output:

```text
Launch pricing recommendation
```

---

# 11. Final Principle

> Pricing steps should turn customer value into a simple offer that can be tested with real buyers.
