# Prepare SaaS Launch — Playbook Definition

Version: 0.1.0  
Status: Premium Draft

---

# 1. Purpose

Guide a structured process for preparing a SaaS product or major feature for launch.

---

# 2. Trigger

A SaaS MVP, beta, pilot, public release or major product feature is approaching launch.

---

# 3. Inputs

Useful inputs include:

- product scope;
- MVP definition;
- PRD;
- launch goals;
- target customers;
- pricing strategy;
- onboarding flow;
- release plan;
- support plan;
- analytics plan;
- launch channels;
- risk register;
- production readiness notes.

---

# 4. Outputs

Expected outputs include:

- launch readiness plan;
- launch goal statement;
- target audience summary;
- product readiness checklist;
- positioning and messaging notes;
- onboarding readiness checklist;
- support readiness checklist;
- launch risk register;
- launch execution checklist;
- post-launch learning plan.

---

# 5. Execution Summary

```text
1. Define launch type and goals
2. Confirm target audience and offer
3. Verify product and release readiness
4. Verify pricing and packaging readiness
5. Verify onboarding and activation path
6. Prepare messaging and launch assets
7. Prepare support and feedback channels
8. Prepare analytics and success metrics
9. Review launch risks and rollback options
10. Execute launch and monitor post-launch signals
```

---

# 6. Completion Criteria

The Playbook is complete when:

- launch goals are defined;
- launch audience is clear;
- product readiness is verified;
- pricing and packaging are ready;
- onboarding path is tested;
- support channels are prepared;
- metrics are instrumented;
- risks and fallback options are documented;
- launch execution owner is assigned.

---

# 7. Escalation or Fallback

Escalate when:

- product readiness is uncertain;
- production hardening is incomplete;
- onboarding blocks user activation;
- pricing is unclear;
- support ownership is missing;
- analytics cannot track launch success;
- security, data or payment risk exists;
- launch date pressure conflicts with quality.

---

# 8. Final Principle

> Launch preparation should reduce uncertainty before the product meets real users.
