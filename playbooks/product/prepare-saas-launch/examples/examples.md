Version: 0.1.0  
Status: Premium Draft

---

# 1. Example — Audiovisual Financing SaaS Beta Launch

## Trigger

A platform that helps creators prepare film, series and documentary projects for financing is ready for private beta.

## Expected Execution

The Playbook should guide the team to:

- define private beta goals;
- select creator and producer segments;
- verify project submission workflow;
- prepare onboarding guide;
- set beta pricing or pilot model;
- track activation and feedback;
- monitor production stability.

## Expected Output

```text
Launch readiness plan
Product readiness checklist
Messaging notes
Support readiness checklist
Launch risk register
Post-launch learning plan
```

# 2. Example — Public MVP Launch

## Trigger

A SaaS MVP is ready for public release.

## Expected Execution

The Playbook should guide the team to:

- confirm pricing and landing page;
- verify signup and onboarding;
- prepare support and FAQ;
- define launch metrics;
- monitor product health after release.

---

# 3. Example — Feature Launch

## Trigger

A major dashboard feature is ready to launch to existing users.

## Expected Execution

The Playbook should guide the team to:

- define audience and rollout plan;
- prepare release notes;
- check feature usage analytics;
- monitor support reports and errors.

---

# 4. Example — Enterprise Pilot Launch

## Trigger

A selected enterprise customer starts a paid pilot.

## Expected Execution

The Playbook should guide the team to:

- define pilot success criteria;
- prepare assisted onboarding;
- confirm support owner;
- define pilot review cadence;
- track conversion signals.

---

# 5. Final Principle

> Examples show that SaaS launch preparation must align product readiness, customer promise and learning system.
