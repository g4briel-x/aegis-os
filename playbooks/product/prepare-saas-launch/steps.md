# Prepare SaaS Launch — Steps

Version: 0.1.0  
Status: Premium Draft

---

# 1. Step 1 — Define Launch Type and Goals

Clarify what kind of launch is being prepared.

Launch types:

```text
private_beta
public_beta
pilot
soft_launch
public_launch
feature_launch
enterprise_launch
```

Capture:

- launch objective;
- target date;
- launch owner;
- audience size;
- success criteria;
- constraints.

Output:

```text
Launch goal statement
```

---

# 2. Step 2 — Confirm Target Audience and Offer

Define who the launch is for and what they receive.

Review:

- target segment;
- buyer;
- user;
- pain point;
- offer;
- pricing;
- activation promise;
- expected outcome.

Output:

```text
Target audience and offer summary
```

---

# 3. Step 3 — Verify Product and Release Readiness

Confirm the product is stable enough for the chosen launch.

Review:

- MVP scope;
- critical workflows;
- release status;
- tests;
- known bugs;
- production hardening;
- performance baseline;
- rollback plan.

Output:

```text
Product readiness checklist result
```

---

# 4. Step 4 — Verify Pricing and Packaging Readiness

Confirm the offer can be understood and purchased or tested.

Review:

- pricing tiers;
- trial or pilot model;
- billing flow;
- upgrade path;
- limits;
- refund or cancellation policy;
- enterprise or custom pricing if relevant.

Output:

```text
Pricing and packaging readiness notes
```

---

# 5. Step 5 — Verify Onboarding and Activation Path

Ensure new users can reach value quickly.

Review:

- signup;
- invitation flow;
- first-run experience;
- empty states;
- tutorial or guide;
- sample data;
- activation milestone;
- support prompts.

Output:

```text
Onboarding readiness checklist result
```

---

# 6. Step 6 — Prepare Messaging and Launch Assets

Create clear communication for the launch.

Assets may include:

- landing page;
- product description;
- announcement post;
- email sequence;
- demo script;
- FAQ;
- pricing page;
- screenshots or video;
- founder note.

Output:

```text
Launch messaging and asset list
```

---

# 7. Step 7 — Prepare Support and Feedback Channels

Define how users will get help and how feedback will be collected.

Review:

- support email;
- chat or form;
- help docs;
- known issue page;
- feedback survey;
- customer interview plan;
- escalation owner.

Output:

```text
Support and feedback readiness plan
```

---

# 8. Step 8 — Prepare Analytics and Success Metrics

Define how launch performance will be measured.

Track:

- visits;
- signups;
- activation;
- conversion;
- retention;
- feature usage;
- error rate;
- support requests;
- revenue or pilot commitments.

Output:

```text
Launch analytics and success metric plan
```

---

# 9. Step 9 — Review Launch Risks and Fallback Options

Identify what can go wrong.

Risks may include:

- onboarding failure;
- production bugs;
- unclear pricing;
- weak acquisition;
- support overload;
- data or security issue;
- payment failure;
- negative user feedback.

Output:

```text
Launch risk register
```

---

# 10. Step 10 — Execute Launch and Monitor Signals

Run the launch and watch early indicators.

Monitor:

- product health;
- signup funnel;
- activation funnel;
- support channels;
- user feedback;
- conversion;
- system errors;
- public response.

Output:

```text
Launch execution and post-launch learning plan
```

---

# 11. Final Principle

> SaaS launch steps should connect readiness, communication, user activation and learning.
