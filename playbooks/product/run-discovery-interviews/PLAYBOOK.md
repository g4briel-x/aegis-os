# Run Discovery Interviews — Playbook Definition

Version: 0.1.0  
Status: Premium Draft

---

# 1. Purpose

Guide a structured customer discovery process from research objective to product decision.

---

# 2. Trigger

A product, feature, market or user problem needs validation through direct customer conversations.

---

# 3. Inputs

Useful inputs include:

- product idea;
- target customer hypothesis;
- problem hypothesis;
- business objective;
- existing user feedback;
- competitor context;
- interview target list;
- assumptions to validate;
- research timeline.

---

# 4. Outputs

Expected outputs include:

- discovery objective;
- participant profile;
- interview script;
- interview notes;
- insight summary;
- assumption validation table;
- pain ranking;
- opportunity recommendation;
- next product decision.

---

# 5. Execution Summary

```text
1. Define discovery objective
2. Identify assumptions
3. Define target participants
4. Prepare interview guide
5. Recruit participants
6. Conduct interviews
7. Capture evidence
8. Synthesize patterns
9. Validate or invalidate assumptions
10. Recommend next product decision
```

---

# 6. Completion Criteria

The Playbook is complete when:

- interviews were conducted with relevant participants;
- notes are captured consistently;
- patterns are synthesized;
- assumptions are validated, invalidated or marked unknown;
- product decision is clearly recommended;
- next action is defined.

---

# 7. Escalation or Fallback

Escalate or adjust when:

- participants do not match the target customer;
- answers are too vague;
- pain signals are weak;
- willingness to pay is unclear;
- the team is biased toward a solution;
- evidence contradicts the original idea.

---

# 8. Final Principle

> Discovery succeeds when it changes or strengthens the product decision with evidence.
