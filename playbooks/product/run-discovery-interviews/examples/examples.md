# 1. Example — Audiovisual SaaS Discovery

## Trigger

A founder wants to validate a SaaS platform that helps film, series and documentary projects move from idea to financing.

## Expected Execution

The Playbook should guide the user to:

- interview creators and producers;
- understand current financing workflow;
- identify pain in pitch preparation, investor access or project packaging;
- detect willingness to pay;
- separate creator pain from investor pain;
- recommend MVP direction.

## Expected Output

```text
Discovery objective
Participant profile
Interview guide
Interview notes
Assumption validation
Insight summary
Product decision recommendation
```

# 2. Example — Freelancer SaaS Discovery

## Trigger

A founder wants to validate a SaaS for freelancers managing clients and invoices.

## Expected Execution

The Playbook should guide the user to:

- interview freelancers in one segment;
- explore current tools;
- understand invoicing pain;
- check willingness to pay;
- determine whether project management or payment tracking is the stronger need.

---

# 3. Example — B2B Workflow SaaS Discovery

## Trigger

A team wants to validate a workflow automation SaaS for small agencies.

## Expected Execution

The Playbook should guide the user to:

- identify agency role and decision maker;
- understand current manual workflow;
- quantify time lost;
- identify adoption blockers;
- test whether the problem has budget.

---

# 4. Example — AI Assistant Discovery

## Trigger

A team wants to validate an AI assistant for business documentation.

## Expected Execution

The Playbook should guide the user to:

- identify current documentation workflow;
- assess trust issues;
- explore quality expectations;
- identify where AI assistance would save time;
- detect concerns about accuracy and confidentiality.

---

# 5. Final Principle

> Examples show that discovery interviews are strongest when they focus on real work, real pain and real decisions.
