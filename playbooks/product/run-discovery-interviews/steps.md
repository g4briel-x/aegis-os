# Run Discovery Interviews — Steps

Version: 0.1.0  
Status: Premium Draft

---

# 1. Step 1 — Define the Discovery Objective

Clarify what the team needs to learn.

Examples:

- validate the pain;
- understand the current workflow;
- identify buying triggers;
- test target segment;
- explore willingness to pay;
- understand adoption blockers.

Output:

```text
Discovery objective
```

---

# 2. Step 2 — List Assumptions

Write assumptions before speaking to participants.

Group assumptions by:

- user segment;
- problem;
- current workaround;
- urgency;
- willingness to pay;
- workflow;
- decision maker;
- adoption risk.

Output:

```text
Assumption list
```

---

# 3. Step 3 — Define Participant Profile

Define who should be interviewed.

Capture:

- role;
- industry;
- company size;
- workflow context;
- pain relevance;
- buying influence;
- accessibility.

Output:

```text
Participant profile
```

---

# 4. Step 4 — Prepare Interview Guide

Prepare questions that explore behavior, not opinions.

Good questions focus on:

- recent real situations;
- current workflow;
- pain intensity;
- existing alternatives;
- cost of the problem;
- decision process;
- constraints.

Output:

```text
Interview guide
```

---

# 5. Step 5 — Recruit Participants

Select participants that match the target profile.

Recommended minimum:

```text
5 interviews for early signal
10 interviews for stronger pattern detection
15+ interviews for segment comparison
```

Output:

```text
Participant list and status
```

---

# 6. Step 6 — Conduct Interviews

During interviews:

- avoid pitching too early;
- ask for examples;
- ask about actual behavior;
- listen for emotion and urgency;
- ask about current alternatives;
- ask what happens if the problem is not solved;
- ask who else is involved in decisions.

Output:

```text
Interview notes
```

---

# 7. Step 7 — Capture Evidence

Record evidence in a structured way.

Capture:

- quote or paraphrased insight;
- pain described;
- workaround;
- frequency;
- severity;
- money or time impact;
- buying signal;
- objection.

Output:

```text
Evidence table
```

---

# 8. Step 8 — Synthesize Patterns

Group findings by repeated themes.

Look for:

- repeated pain;
- repeated workflow;
- repeated objections;
- repeated willingness-to-pay signals;
- repeated language;
- unexpected user segment.

Output:

```text
Pattern summary
```

---

# 9. Step 9 — Validate Assumptions

Mark each assumption as:

```text
validated
invalidated
partially validated
unknown
```

Output:

```text
Assumption validation table
```

---

# 10. Step 10 — Recommend Product Decision

Make a decision recommendation.

Possible outcomes:

- proceed to MVP definition;
- refine target segment;
- pivot problem framing;
- run more interviews;
- prototype before building;
- stop or deprioritize.

Output:

```text
Product decision recommendation
```

---

# 11. Final Principle

> Discovery steps should reveal what customers actually do, not what they politely say.
