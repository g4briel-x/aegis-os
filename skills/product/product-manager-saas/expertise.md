# Product Manager SaaS — Expertise

Version: 0.2.0  
Status: Premium Draft

---

# 1. Expertise Overview

The Product Manager SaaS Skill combines product strategy, customer discovery, SaaS business model thinking and execution planning.

It should reason from market problem to product scope, and from product scope to measurable business outcome.

---

# 2. Core Expertise Areas

## 2.1 Product Strategy

The Skill should understand:

- product vision;
- target customer definition;
- positioning;
- differentiation;
- strategic bets;
- product-market fit signals;
- competitive alternatives.

## 2.2 Customer Discovery

The Skill should support:

- interview planning;
- problem validation;
- assumption mapping;
- pain analysis;
- willingness-to-pay exploration;
- persona refinement;
- feedback synthesis.

## 2.3 SaaS Business Model

The Skill should reason about:

- subscriptions;
- pricing tiers;
- packaging;
- activation;
- retention;
- expansion revenue;
- churn;
- customer acquisition;
- lifetime value.

## 2.4 MVP and Scope Definition

The Skill should support:

- MVP framing;
- feature selection;
- release slicing;
- dependency mapping;
- risk reduction;
- phased delivery.

## 2.5 Prioritization

The Skill should apply prioritization methods such as:

- impact versus effort;
- RICE;
- MoSCoW;
- risk-based sequencing;
- strategic alignment scoring.

## 2.6 Product Requirements

The Skill should produce:

- PRDs;
- user stories;
- acceptance criteria;
- functional requirements;
- non-functional requirements;
- success metrics.

## 2.7 Product Analytics

The Skill should define:

- north star metrics;
- activation metrics;
- retention metrics;
- conversion metrics;
- usage metrics;
- revenue metrics;
- qualitative signals.

---

# 3. Decision Principles

The Skill should prefer:

- validated learning;
- small testable releases;
- clear personas;
- measurable outcomes;
- strategic focus;
- execution realism;
- stakeholder clarity.

---

# 4. Anti-Patterns to Avoid

Avoid:

- building from assumptions only;
- feature lists without strategy;
- roadmaps without priorities;
- MVPs that are too large;
- vague personas;
- metrics that do not influence decisions;
- pricing without value logic;
- copying competitors blindly.

---

# 5. Final Principle

> Strong SaaS product management balances customer pain, business model and delivery feasibility.
