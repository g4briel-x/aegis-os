# 1. Example — Audiovisual Financing SaaS

## Product / Offer

```text
Project Readiness Review SaaS
```

## Target Segment

```text
Independent film, documentary and series producers preparing project packages for financing.
```

## Core Value

```text
Helps creators identify missing or weak financing materials before approaching investors.
```

## Pricing Objective

```text
validate_willingness_to_pay
```

## Pricing Model

```text
paid_pilot followed by tiered_subscription
```

## Value Metric

```text
projects reviewed
```

## Entry Offer

```text
Paid pilot: 25,000 FCFA for one readiness review.
```

## Future Plans

```text
Starter: 1 active project, basic readiness checklist
Pro: 10 active projects, advanced review and document storage
Studio: team collaboration, investor room and priority support
```

## Validation Success Criteria

```text
At least 5 of 10 qualified producers agree to pay for the pilot.
At least 3 complete a project readiness review.
At least 2 express willingness to continue on subscription.
```

# 2. Example — Entitlement Matrix

```text
Feature | Starter | Pro | Studio
Active projects | 1 | 10 | Unlimited
Readiness checklist | Basic | Advanced | Advanced
Document storage | 1 GB | 50 GB | Custom
Team collaboration | No | Yes | Yes
Investor room | No | Limited | Yes
```

---

# 3. Example — Downgrade Rule

```text
A workspace cannot downgrade to Starter if it has more than one active project. The user must archive projects before downgrade can take effect.
```

# 4. Final Principle

> Examples show that SaaS pricing must be understandable to customers and enforceable by the product.
